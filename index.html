[Advent of Code](https://adventofcode.com) challenges exercise various aspects of raw coding, compared to LeetCode which focuses on classical algorithms. After raw coding, you won't **need** to grind LeetCode since you'll already be a great coder. ### {{ faSolid("microchip") }} Low-Level Computing: Assembly & C {{ renderMeterBar("Beginner", "Staff") }} ASM is the underpinning of all programming languages, and learning it removes all the mystery, the fog of war that was there in terms of how software really works. When you understand how every programming language actually under the hood, the differences between them matter less. It's never too early or too late to get into assembly. In many ways, assembly code is simpler than high-level code since there are fewer concepts at the core layer. An assembly language curriculum for a beginner will be basic programming, whereas for someone advanced will be much more involved, mapping what they already know about computers onto the low-level. ### {{ faSolid("lambda") }} Pure Functional Programming With Haskell {{ renderMeterBar("Beginner", "Staff") }} It's also never too early or too late to get into Haskell. Pure functional programming is a different paradigm, and Haskell is the only language that forces you to stay within the functional paradigm: no loops, no reassignment. At first your head will spin as you figure out how to restructure imperative code you already know how to write. It will feel like relearning coding from the beginning. And then you'll internalize the [Church-Turing Equivalence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Church%E2%80%93Turing_thesis) into your whole concept of coding and computation. ### {{ faSolid("terminal") }} UNIX {{ renderMeterBar("Junior", "Staff") }} A solid understanding of UNIX is an absolute must, if you're interested in learning about devops, containers or deployment, but also just in terms of using your own computer more effectively. Learn your way around the Linux filesystem, user permissions, commands and pipe chains, and increase your mastery over your whole computer. ### {{ faSolid("cogs") }} Web Framework From Scratch {{ renderMeterBar("Junior", "Staff") }} I'm not going to say don't use a web framework, that would be crazy. I will argue though that understanding how the web, HTTP, and the frameworks you use work, and having the skills to make one yourself will greatly increase your abilities to build and debug code. On most teams, there's one person who understands this stuff, while the rest are at the framework level, treating it like a black box. That one person does all the hard stuff, and fixes all the bugs. That's how it goes, the more you know, the more you can do. ### {{ faSolid("atom") }} Neural Networks From Scratch {{ renderMeterBar("Junior", "Staff") }} A short and simple introduction to building and training neural networks, and backpropagation.
